Introduction to nootropics

Nootropics are substances, often in the form of supplements (but can be derived from food) that individuals use to potentially enhance cognitive function which people report benefits such as improved memory, increased focus, increased productivity, the ability to learn new skills quickly and reduced feelings of stress which is likely due to lowered levels of the stress hormone cortisol.

Unlocking the Power of Nootropics

Nootropics are referred to as "cognitive enhancers," are chemicals, vitamins, medications, or other compounds that are intended to help healthy people with their cognitive function, especially with memory, creativity, or motivation. These substances are used to improve mental function, heighten awareness, and maybe improve brain health in general.

Dr. Corneliu E. Giurgea, a Romanian psychologist and chemist, is credited with creating the word "nootropic" and defining the requirements by which a chemical meets these requirements. Among these requirements are:

  • Improving memory and learning.
  • Shielding the brain from toxins or physical harm.
  • Demonstrating minimal adverse effects and low toxicity.
  • Improving the neuronal firing control systems' effectiveness.
